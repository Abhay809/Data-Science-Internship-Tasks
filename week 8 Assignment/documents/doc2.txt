Title: Overview of Cricket Rules and Formats

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of 11 players each. The game is played on a large oval field, with a 22-yard-long pitch at the center. One team bats while the other bowls and fields. The objective is to score more runs than the opponent.

Key Formats:
1. Test Cricket:
   - Duration: Up to 5 days
   - Innings: 2 per side
   - Traditional and most strategic form of the game
   - Played with a red ball

2. One Day International (ODI):
   - 50 overs per side
   - Played in a single day
   - White ball, colored uniforms
   - Popular format in World Cups

3. Twenty20 (T20):
   - 20 overs per side
   - High-intensity, short format
   - Emphasis on aggressive batting
   - Tournaments include IPL, BBL, PSL

Basic Rules:
- Each over consists of 6 legal deliveries.
- Batsmen score runs by running between the wickets or hitting boundaries.
- A batsman can be out in several ways: bowled, caught, LBW, run out, stumped.
- A bowler can bowl a maximum of 10 overs in ODIs, and 4 in T20s.

Fielding Positions:
- Common positions include slip, gully, point, cover, mid-off, mid-on, square leg, and fine leg.

Umpires:
- Two on-field umpires make decisions with support from a third umpire using DRS (Decision Review System).

Cricket is governed internationally by the ICC (International Cricket Council), which sets the rules and organizes major tournaments like the World Cup and the Champions Trophy.
